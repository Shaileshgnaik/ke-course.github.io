    Part 1 — Foundations of Knowledge Engineering¶

    "Understanding the WHY before the HOW"

    Duration: ~6–8 hours | Modules: 5 | Labs: 5 | Prerequisites: None

    Module Title Key Topics
    1.1 What is Knowledge Engineering? Definition, history, KE vs ML vs Gen AI
    1.2 Types of Knowledge Explicit/tacit, declarative/procedural, shallow/deep, certain/uncertain
    1.3 The KE Lifecycle Acquire → Represent → Validate → Deploy → Maintain
    1.4 Expert Systems Architecture, MYCIN/DENDRAL/XCON, inference, explanation
    1.5 The Acquisition Bottleneck Why it's hard, tacit knowledge, modern solutions

    Go to Part 1 →

    Part 2 — Knowledge Acquisition¶

    "Getting knowledge OUT of expert minds"

    Duration: ~6 hours | Modules: 5

    Module Title Key Topics
    2.1 Knowledge Elicitation Techniques Structured interviews, think-aloud, observation, Delphi method
    2.2 Document & Text Mining NLP extraction, Named Entity Recognition, Relation Extraction
    2.3 ML for Knowledge Acquisition Decision tree induction, association rules
    2.4 Knowledge Acquisition from LLMs Prompt engineering for elicitation, validating LLM knowledge
    2.5 Practical Lab Full elicitation exercise on a case study domain

    Part 3 — Knowledge Representation¶

    "Giving knowledge a structure machines can use"

    Duration: ~8 hours | Modules: 7

    Module Title Key Topics
    3.1 Introduction to Representation Criteria: expressiveness, efficiency, maintainability
    3.2 Logic-Based Representation Propositional logic, First-Order logic, Horn clauses
    3.3 Rule-Based Representation IF-THEN rules, conflict resolution — Drools, CLIPS
    3.4 Semantic Networks & Frames Nodes, arcs, IS-A, HAS-A hierarchies
    3.5 Ontologies OWL, RDF, Protégé — build your first ontology
    3.6 Knowledge Graphs Triples, Neo4j, Amazon Neptune, Azure Cosmos DB
    3.7 Probabilistic Representation Bayesian networks, fuzzy logic, uncertainty handling

    Part 4 — Knowledge Base Design & Storage¶

    "Architecting the brain of your system"

    Duration: ~5 hours | Modules: 5

    Module Title Key Topics
    4.1 KB Architecture Fact base vs rule base vs case base
    4.2 Storage Technologies Graph DBs, triple stores, relational + document stores
    4.3 Knowledge Base Quality Completeness, consistency, correctness, currency
    4.4 Versioning & Governance Change management, audit trails, version control for KB
    4.5 Lab: Design a Knowledge Base Hands-on KB design in Neo4j or Apache Jena

    Part 5 — Inference & Reasoning¶

    "Teaching the system to THINK"

    Duration: ~7 hours | Modules: 6

    Module Title Key Topics
    5.1 Introduction to Inference Deductive, inductive, abductive reasoning
    5.2 Forward Chaining Data-driven reasoning, Rete algorithm
    5.3 Backward Chaining Goal-driven reasoning, Prolog-style
    5.4 Case-Based Reasoning Retrieve, Reuse, Revise, Retain (4Rs)
    5.5 Uncertainty & Probabilistic Reasoning Certainty factors, Bayesian inference
    5.6 Explainability HOW/WHY explanations, GDPR, building explainable AI

    Part 6 — KE + Artificial Intelligence¶

    "The Modern Hybrid: Combining Symbolic and Neural AI"

    Duration: ~7 hours | Modules: 6

    Module Title Key Topics
    6.1 Symbolic vs Connectionist AI Rule-based vs neural — why neither alone is enough
    6.2 Neuro-Symbolic AI Knowledge-infused learning, AlphaGo, AlphaFold
    6.3 KE + LLMs RAG + KB, Knowledge Graphs as LLM memory
    6.4 Ontology-Driven AI Agents Structured knowledge in AI agents
    6.5 Vector DBs vs Knowledge Graphs Semantic similarity vs structured reasoning
    6.6 KE for Prompt Engineering Dynamic knowledge injection, domain expert prompts

    Part 7 — KE System Architecture & Implementation¶

    "Building production-grade KE systems"

    Duration: ~6 hours | Modules: 6

    Module Title Key Topics
    7.1 KE Architecture Patterns Standalone, microservice, cloud-native KE
    7.2 Cloud Implementation Azure (Cognitive Services, Logic Apps) · AWS (Kendra, Neptune, Lambda)
    7.3 KE Pipeline Design Ingestion → Inference → Feedback loop
    7.4 Performance & Scalability Rule engine optimization, distributed KB
    7.5 Testing a KE System Unit testing rules, regression testing KB
    7.6 Security & Governance Access control, audit logging, data privacy

    Part 8 — Real-World Applications & Capstone¶

    "Applying KE to solve real problems"

    Duration: ~8 hours | Modules: 7

    Module Title Domain
    8.1 KE in Healthcare Clinical decision support, SNOMED-CT, UMLS
    8.2 KE in Finance & Compliance Regulatory rules, fraud detection, export compliance
    8.3 KE in Cloud & IT Architecture Architecture recommendation engines
    8.4 KE in Customer Experience Intelligent chatbots, product knowledge graphs
    8.5 KE in Manufacturing & IoT Predictive maintenance, fault diagnosis
    8.6 Capstone Project 1 Full KE Expert System — end to end
    8.7 Capstone Project 2 KE + LLM Hybrid App on Azure or AWS
