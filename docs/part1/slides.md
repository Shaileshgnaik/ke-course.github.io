## Module 1.1 Slide Outline {#module-11-slide-outline}
**"What is Knowledge Engineering?"** | ~10 slides

---

**Slide 1 — Title**
- Headline: *What is Knowledge Engineering?*
- Subtitle: Module 1.1 — Foundations of KE
- Visual: Brain → Gears → Computer (transformation image)

---

**Slide 2 — Opening Hook**
- Headline: *"A retiring expert takes 30 years of knowledge with them. What if they didn't have to?"*
- Visual: Senior expert handing over an empty box
- Points:
    - Expertise lives in people, not systems
    - What happens when experts leave?
    - KE solves this problem

---

**Slide 3 — Definition**
- Headline: *What is Knowledge Engineering?*
- Visual: 3-box flow diagram (Capture → Represent → Apply)
- Quote: *"The discipline of CAPTURING, STRUCTURING, and APPLYING human expertise so computers can reason, decide, and solve problems — with explainability"*
- Callout: *KE = Bridge between Human Expertise & Machine AI*

---

**Slide 4 — The Recipe Analogy**
- Headline: *"Teaching a robot Grandma's secret recipe"*
- Visual: Grandma (left) → Robot with question mark (right)
- Two-column table:
    - Grandma says → KE translates to
    - "A pinch of love" → "5g salt, stir 3 mins at 80°C"
    - "Bake till right" → "180°C for exactly 25 minutes"

---

**Slide 5 — KE vs Other Approaches**
- Headline: *KE Is Not the Same as ML or Gen AI*
- Visual: Comparison table (4 columns)
- Columns: Traditional Prog | ML | Gen AI | KE
- Rows: Knowledge Source, Explainability, Data Needed, Best For
- Callout: *"KE is the backbone that makes AI trustworthy"*

---

**Slide 6 — History Timeline**
- Headline: *60 Years of Knowledge Engineering*
- Visual: Horizontal timeline with icons per decade
- Points: 1960s DENDRAL → 1970s MYCIN → 1980s Commercial boom → 1990s Semantic Web → 2000s Knowledge Graphs → 2020s KE + LLMs

---

**Slide 7 — The Modern Combo**
- Headline: *The Future = KE + Gen AI + ML*
- Visual: Three overlapping circles
    - Gen AI: Flexibility, language
    - KE: Accuracy, explainability
    - ML: Pattern learning, scale
- Callout: *Overlap = Production-grade trustworthy AI*

---

**Slide 8 — Key Takeaways**
- Headline: *What We Learned*
- Visual: 5 numbered icons
    1. KE captures and applies human expertise
    2. Born from Expert Systems in the 1960s
    3. Different from ML: rule-based vs data-driven
    4. Different from Gen AI: precise vs probabilistic
    5. Future = KE + Gen AI combined

## Module 1.2 Slide Outline {#module-12-slide-outline}
**"Types of Knowledge"** | ~9 slides

---

**Slide 1 — Title**
- Headline: *Types of Knowledge*
- Subtitle: Not all knowledge is created equal
- Visual: Iceberg image

---

**Slide 2 — The Iceberg Model**
- Headline: *Most Valuable Knowledge is Hidden Below the Surface*
- Visual: Iceberg diagram with labels
    - Above water: Explicit Knowledge (written rules, manuals, SOPs)
    - Below water: Tacit Knowledge (intuition, experience, judgment)
- Callout: *"KE's hardest job: surfacing what's underwater"*

---

**Slide 3 — Explicit vs Tacit**
- Headline: *Explicit vs Tacit Knowledge*
- Visual: Side-by-side with real examples
    - Explicit: "Blood pressure > 140 = hypertension"
    - Tacit: Doctor "senses" something is wrong
- Footer: How to capture each type

---

**Slide 4 — Declarative vs Procedural**
- Headline: *WHAT vs HOW*
- Visual: Two-column layout with icons
    - Declarative: Facts, Concepts, IS-A relationships
    - Procedural: Steps, Processes, HOW-TO logic
- Example: Medical diagnosis facts vs diagnostic steps

---

**Slide 5 — Shallow vs Deep Knowledge**
- Headline: *Surface Pattern vs Root Cause Understanding*
- Visual: Two brain diagrams — surface scan vs deep scan
    - Shallow: Pattern match — "Symptom X → Disease Y"
    - Deep: Causal — "Why X causes Y at the cellular level"
- Callout: *"Deep knowledge handles edge cases. Shallow fails."*

---

**Slide 6 — Certain vs Uncertain Knowledge**
- Headline: *Real-World Knowledge is Rarely Black & White*
- Visual: Certainty spectrum bar
    - Left: 100% Certain (water boils at 100°C)
    - Right: Uncertain (patient probably has X — 85%)
- Tools listed: Certainty factors, Bayesian networks, Fuzzy logic

---

**Slide 7 — The Knowledge Pyramid**
- Headline: *Data → Information → Knowledge → Expertise → Wisdom*
- Visual: Pyramid with each level labelled
- Callout: *"KE operates at the Knowledge-to-Expertise level"*

---

**Slide 8 — Practical Examples**
- Headline: *Classify These Statements from a Cloud Architect*
- Visual: 5 statements as cards, audience classifies each
- Use as an interactive exercise during live sessions

---

**Slide 9 — Key Takeaways**
- 5 bullet points: explicit/tacit, declarative/procedural, shallow/deep, certain/uncertain, pyramid
