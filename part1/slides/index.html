    Part 1 — Slide Outlines¶

    These outlines provide the structure for each module's presentation slides. Each slide entry lists: Headline, Visual, and Key Points. Use these with PowerPoint, Google Slides, or any presentation tool.

    Module 1.1 Slide Outline¶

    "What is Knowledge Engineering?" | ~10 slides

    Slide 1 — Title - Headline: What is Knowledge Engineering? - Subtitle: Module 1.1 — Foundations of KE - Visual: Brain → Gears → Computer (transformation image)

    Slide 2 — Opening Hook - Headline: "A retiring expert takes 30 years of knowledge with them. What if they didn't have to?" - Visual: Senior expert handing over an empty box - Points: - Expertise lives in people, not systems - What happens when experts leave? - KE solves this problem

    Slide 3 — Definition - Headline: What is Knowledge Engineering? - Visual: 3-box flow diagram (Capture → Represent → Apply) - Quote: "The discipline of CAPTURING, STRUCTURING, and APPLYING human expertise so computers can reason, decide, and solve problems — with explainability" - Callout: KE = Bridge between Human Expertise & Machine AI

    Slide 4 — The Recipe Analogy - Headline: "Teaching a robot Grandma's secret recipe" - Visual: Grandma (left) → Robot with question mark (right) - Two-column table: - Grandma says → KE translates to - "A pinch of love" → "5g salt, stir 3 mins at 80°C" - "Bake till right" → "180°C for exactly 25 minutes"

    Slide 5 — KE vs Other Approaches - Headline: KE Is Not the Same as ML or Gen AI - Visual: Comparison table (4 columns) - Columns: Traditional Prog | ML | Gen AI | KE - Rows: Knowledge Source, Explainability, Data Needed, Best For - Callout: "KE is the backbone that makes AI trustworthy"

    Slide 6 — History Timeline - Headline: 60 Years of Knowledge Engineering - Visual: Horizontal timeline with icons per decade - Points: 1960s DENDRAL → 1970s MYCIN → 1980s Commercial boom → 1990s Semantic Web → 2000s Knowledge Graphs → 2020s KE + LLMs

    Slide 7 — The Modern Combo - Headline: The Future = KE + Gen AI + ML - Visual: Three overlapping circles - Gen AI: Flexibility, language - KE: Accuracy, explainability - ML: Pattern learning, scale - Callout: Overlap = Production-grade trustworthy AI

    Slide 8 — Key Takeaways - Headline: What We Learned - Visual: 5 numbered icons 1. KE captures and applies human expertise 2. Born from Expert Systems in the 1960s 3. Different from ML: rule-based vs data-driven 4. Different from Gen AI: precise vs probabilistic 5. Future = KE + Gen AI combined

    Module 1.2 Slide Outline¶

    "Types of Knowledge" | ~9 slides

    Slide 1 — Title - Headline: Types of Knowledge - Subtitle: Not all knowledge is created equal - Visual: Iceberg image

    Slide 2 — The Iceberg Model - Headline: Most Valuable Knowledge is Hidden Below the Surface - Visual: Iceberg diagram with labels - Above water: Explicit Knowledge (written rules, manuals, SOPs) - Below water: Tacit Knowledge (intuition, experience, judgment) - Callout: "KE's hardest job: surfacing what's underwater"

    Slide 3 — Explicit vs Tacit - Headline: Explicit vs Tacit Knowledge - Visual: Side-by-side with real examples - Explicit: "Blood pressure > 140 = hypertension" - Tacit: Doctor "senses" something is wrong - Footer: How to capture each type

    Slide 4 — Declarative vs Procedural - Headline: WHAT vs HOW - Visual: Two-column layout with icons - Declarative: Facts, Concepts, IS-A relationships - Procedural: Steps, Processes, HOW-TO logic - Example: Medical diagnosis facts vs diagnostic steps

    Slide 5 — Shallow vs Deep Knowledge - Headline: Surface Pattern vs Root Cause Understanding - Visual: Two brain diagrams — surface scan vs deep scan - Shallow: Pattern match — "Symptom X → Disease Y" - Deep: Causal — "Why X causes Y at the cellular level" - Callout: "Deep knowledge handles edge cases. Shallow fails."

    Slide 6 — Certain vs Uncertain Knowledge - Headline: Real-World Knowledge is Rarely Black & White - Visual: Certainty spectrum bar - Left: 100% Certain (water boils at 100°C) - Right: Uncertain (patient probably has X — 85%) - Tools listed: Certainty factors, Bayesian networks, Fuzzy logic

    Slide 7 — The Knowledge Pyramid - Headline: Data → Information → Knowledge → Expertise → Wisdom - Visual: Pyramid with each level labelled - Callout: "KE operates at the Knowledge-to-Expertise level"

    Slide 8 — Practical Examples - Headline: Classify These Statements from a Cloud Architect - Visual: 5 statements as cards, audience classifies each - Use as an interactive exercise during live sessions

    Slide 9 — Key Takeaways - 5 bullet points: explicit/tacit, declarative/procedural, shallow/deep, certain/uncertain, pyramid

    Slide 2 — What is an Expert System? - Formula: Expert System = KB + Inference Engine + Explanation Module - Callout: "It can explain its reasoning — unlike black-box AI"

    Slide 3 — Classic Examples - 4 cards: DENDRAL, MYCIN, XCON, PROSPECTOR - Per card: System | Year | Domain | Key achievement

    Slide 4 — Architecture - Full architecture diagram with all 6 components - Arrows showing data flow

    Slide 5 — How It Works: The Medical Example - 6-step numbered flow: Input → Search KB → Fire Rules → Calculate CF → Output → Explain

    Slide 6 — Forward vs Backward Chaining - Two columns side by side with visual flow - When to use each

    Slide 7 — Certainty Factors - Headline: Handling Uncertainty the MYCIN Way - Formula: Combined CF = CF1 × CF2 - Example calculation

    Slide 8 — Strengths vs Limitations - Green column (strengths) | Red column (limitations) - Footer: "These limitations shaped modern KE"

    Module 1.5 Slide Outline¶

    "The Knowledge Acquisition Bottleneck" | ~8 slides

    Slide 1 — Title: The Knowledge Acquisition Bottleneck

    Slide 2 — The Core Problem - Headline: "I Just Know" — The Expert's Dilemma - Visual: Full expert head (left) → Empty knowledge base (right) with broken arrow

    Slide 3 — The Bicycle Test - Headline: You Know How to Ride. Can You Explain It? - Visual: Person on bicycle with thought bubbles showing subconscious adjustments

    Slide 4 — 5 Reasons It's Hard - 5 icons with short labels - Tacit subconscious | Over-simplification | Context-dependence | Expert disagreement | Fear of replacement

    Slide 5 — Think-Aloud in Action - Headline: Capture Real-Time Reasoning, Not Retrospective Explanation - Side-by-side: Bad interview vs Think-Aloud protocol - Quote from expert narrating their own thinking

    Slide 6 — 5 Modern Solutions - 5 cards: ML from data | Think-aloud | Case-based | LLMs | Rule discovery

    Slide 7 — Human-AI Collaborative KE - 4-step flow: AI extracts → Expert reviews → AI formalizes → Expert validates
